Legal Issues

The numerous legal issues plaguing general web development

Such as the internet has grown to be an almost ubiquitous presence in our lives, so has the legality associated with it. It's relative mystery to the average user leaves open many vectors of attack through methods like Phishing and Keylogging; also the breadth of the internet means that even if content was stolen it is unlikely the creator would ever see it. Below is the legality associated and the methods taken to prevent them being broken.

Free Images off the Internet

You cannot simply take images from an internet search for use, commercial or otherwise. For example, you cannot take an image from Google Images and assume it is legal to use.
Images are fingerprinted which means that if it is found on a site without permission the image is flagged and the content owner is notified.
Again, this opens you up to legal recourse

Obscene Publications Act

Started in 1959, the Obscene Publications Act is an act of parliament in England and Wales that reformed the publishing of any obscene content. This was to make laws stronger against things such as ‘Pornography.’ There have been many prosecutions that have been under the act. Such as:

The Lady Chatterley's Lover trial
The Stass Paraskos trial

Accessibility

Web accessibility is the inclusive practice of ensuring there are no barriers that prevent interaction with, or access to websites, by people with disabilities. When sites are correctly designed, developed and edited, generally all users have equal access to information and functionality. This is done by means of "alt" tags on images to describe what the image represents for people such as the blind or languge settings to help search engines categorise search results.
