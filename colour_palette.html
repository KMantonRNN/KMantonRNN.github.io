Colour Palettes

The different ways to specify colour

Another very important element of Web Design is the choice of colours.

The first thing we must consider is that there are strong colours, which draw the attention of the user and encourage interaction; these colours are to be reserved for important elements such as checkout buttons or download buttons. Under no circumstance should the background of the page use a strong colour. By doing this you will make it very difficult for the user to find any other important elements on the page.

In contrast to strong colours are soft colours. These colours are perfect for general styling and background colours. However, they must be consistent accross the entire website. This helps the user remain in control of the pages they visit. If each page were to be styled differently they would perhaps assume they have even left the site!

Below are a variety of CSS methods of applying colours to elements, ranging from simple methods such as colour names to methods with minute levels of detail.

RGB

Red, Green, Blue

Values between 0 and 255

Hexadecimal

RR(red), GG(green) and BB(blue)
#RRGGBB
Hexadecimal integers between 00 and FF
