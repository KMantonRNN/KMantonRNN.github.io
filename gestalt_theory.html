Gestalt Theory

An important concept in Web Design

Sourced via spokenefalls.edu

The concepts within the Gestalt Theory are perhaps the most relevant and important they have ever been. Modern web design is about being on the bleeding edge of design and these 6 principles, when applied, inspire awe in the user and emit an aura of professionalism and thoughtfulness. When we also consider the emphasis it gives to an image/element we can say it proves invaluable at capturing the user's attention.

Having the ability to control the user's focus via the use of well placed elements allows us to create a path through the site to help them navigate and find things, amongst other.

Similarity

Occurs when objects look similar to each other causing the viewer to perceive an image or a pattern.

Dissimilarity

Occurs when an anomalous object is present in similar objects. This creates emphasis.

Continuation

Occurs when the eye is compelled to move through one object and continue to another. Often through the use of white-space.

Closure

Closure occurs when an object is incomplete or a space is not completely enclosed. People perceive the whole by filling in the missing information.

Figure and Ground

A form, silhouette or shape is naturally percieved as a figure/object while the surrounding area is percieved as the ground (background). This adds interest and subtlety to an image.

Proximity

Proximity occurs when objects are placed close together and are therefore percieved as a group.
